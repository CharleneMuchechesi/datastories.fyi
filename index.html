A Seat at the Table

Nine of sixteen Southern African countries now have more girls than boys enrolled in secondary school. That sounds like a victory — until you look at what happens next. Angola's female primary completion rate sits at 34%. Mozambique's at 51%. Enrollment is not the same as staying. This is the story the headline numbers don't tell.
